History

The Sukhoi design bureau was founded in 1939 and has produced a range of military aircraft. The Su-27 (Flanker) family entered service in the 1980s and remains one of the most capable fighter families in the world.

It played a key role in the Soviet Air Force and later the Russian Aerospace Forces.

Development

The Su-27 was developed to counter the U.S. fourth-generation fighters such as the F-15 Eagle. Over the years, it has been upgraded with newer avionics, engines, weapons systems and variants tailored for export and domestic roles.

Variants

  • Su-27 – original single-seat air superiority fighter
  • Su-30 – two-seat multirole version
  • Su-33 – naval carrier-based version
  • Su-34 – two-seat strike/fighter-bomber version
  • Su-35 – advanced single-seat super-maneuverable version
  • Su-57 – fifth-generation stealth fighter (planned successor)

Legacy & Influence

The Sukhoi family has influenced aircraft design worldwide and remains in active service with several air forces. Its performance and versatility have secured its position in both national defence and export markets.
